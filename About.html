ABOUT US

IBW is a Lesotho company that operates under the supervision of co-founder Kenneth. The company started in 2024 with a capital of M100000. During his journey on sourcing funds, Kenneth partnered with Shadrack, who joined as a second CEO of the company. The mandate of IBW is to recycle computer parts ; mainly the RAM( Random Access Memory),HDs(hard Drives) and components of the motherboard. In 2025, the company penetrated the market and became one of the pioneers of electronic recycling int the Southern Region of Africa. The growth of IBW attracted investors, parners and clients outside of Lesotho.

A recycling company of softwares" essentially refers to a company that specializes in the process of reusing and repurposing old software code, often by extracting valuable components from outdated software applications to create new software products, contributing to a more sustainable software development approach; a prominent example of this is the company AMCS Group, which offers a cloud-based recycling software platform designed to streamline and automate operations for various recycling businesses, including managing collection routes, inventory, billing, and customer interactions, allowing for efficient recycling processes and data analysis. Key points about software recycling companies: Focus on reuse: They aim to salvage usable code from old software systems to avoid writing new code from scratch whenever possible. Component-based approach: Often break down old software into reusable components like functions, modules, or libraries.
